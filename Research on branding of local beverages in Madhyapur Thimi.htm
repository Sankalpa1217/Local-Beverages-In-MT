Research on branding of local beverages in Madhyapur Thimi

 

INTRODUCTION

Newa, the indigenous people of Maddhyapur Thimi are well-known for their culture, traditions, lifestyles, food along with several alcoholic beverages as well. Alcohol preparation and consumption are integral parts of their culture. Popular local drinks such as Thwon and Ayla have the potential to be branded along ethnic lines. These beverages have their own distinct flavor and aura, and they are uniquely associated with ethnic communities.

Ayla is a strong beverage: a thrilling and smooth grain alcohol. Rice aila has a rich and smooth flavor, whereas kodo or millet has a stronger and more fiery flavor. The ingredients are selected  then combined with marcha (an edible organic compound used for fermentation). The fermentation process takes four to five days. The fermented mixture is known locally as Thwon and it can be consumed as a raw and strong liquor.Ayla preparation usually requires  a set of clay and brass vessels created specifically for this purpose. These vessels are known as 'phosi,' 'pottasi,duguli and 'aila bata. Fermented rice is poured into the phosi, which is then placed over a wood fire, for the distilling process. A potasi with numerous holes in its base is placed on top of the phosi. The vapor passes through these holes and is cooled by the cold water in the aila bata. The aila bata, a brass vessel, is placed on top of the potasi and cools the vapor, converting it to liquid droplets. These liquid droplets congregate in the duguli, which is placed inside potasi.And finally aila is collected in duguli.

Manaa and marcha

Manaa and marcha are the starter culture for the preparation of fermented beverages Ayla and Thwon respectively. Till now, local people themselves are involved in the production of these starter culture.

Basically, Marcha is prepared from soaked rice with some wild herbs, ginger and dry red-chilli and a bit of previously crushed marcha powder. However, the actual composition is still unknown.

Similarly, manaa is prepared from wheat grains. Firstly, wheat grains are boiled and pressed. Then, molds are allowed to grow and dried.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

        Huge amount of Nepalese currency is flowing outside the country in importing foreign branded beverages.

        Taste of local beverages varies with each batch.

        Raw materials used for fermentation like rice being a staple in our country, there are some restrictions to use on a larger scale.

        Unstandardized methods of preparation of the beverages causes the possibility of alcohol poisoning.

        No significant research and literature exists on the properties, standards and characterization of nepali household Marcha.

OBJECTIVES

        To provide global exposure to a well formulated nepali beverage.

        To promote and uplift the local Newa community by modernizing the traditional way of making alcohol without losing the local flavors.

        To maintain taste, quality and uniformity throughout the batches of beverages produced while maintaining its traditional value. 

        To standardize the starter(marcha) for a better and refined product.

METHEDOLOGY (PROJECT DESIGN PLAN)

The project will be divided into four work packages:

Community engagement

Interaction and discussion about the traditional process of marcha making with local people of community (raw material, process involved, time required, factor affecting, etc)

Sampling of starter culture and their characterization

Isolation of microbes from marcha and their characterization and preservation. Characterization is based on physical, chemical and biological aspects of microbes.

As we know, different microorganisms need different factors in order to grow and survive. Such factors are physical, chemical and biological properties that define and determine the optimal growth environment for the microbes. The most important physical factors are pH, temperature, pressure, oxygen availability and salinity

Similarly, different microbes have different requirements for nutrients as well as the amount of each nutrient that they must acquire. Macro and micro nutrients such as carbon, hydrogen, oxygen nitrogen, phosphorus, sulfur, magnanes, zinc, cobalt molybdenum etc. 

 

Standardization of marcha

Marcha consists of numerous microorganisms coexisting in the culture. Although the characterization has been attempted, there is no evidence of the standard quantification of the Marcha, especially that of Nepali Marcha. This project will work on standardization of Marcha in two phases:

Phase I : Determination of ratio of yeast to mold to bacteria following up after the characterization process.

Phase II: To prepare the combination of different microorganisms and hit and trial the combination to assess and compare the quality of fermentation of each combination. 

 

Check the compatibility of micro-organism

The compatibility of micro-organisms are crucial for formulating the inoculants as it is important that they exist synergistically in the starter culture. Antagonism amongst microorganisms is a common phenomenon. The formulation of the best combination of microorganisms needs to be followed up with a compatibility test that the microorganisms exist in a mutualistic relationship for a long period of shelf time. 

Scaling up the Marcha Making and Fermentation process

For proper scaling up and large-scale production of traditional alcoholic beverages, the marcha production must also be similarly scaled. Production of high quality marcha cake is largely dependent on the standarization of the microbial growth, along with the selection of optimal consortia for maximum efficiency. Since marcha is generally produced at-home in small to medium scales, large-scale production will require industrial-grade machinery and production systems.

For the fermentation and production of traditional alcoholic beverages, special focus must be given to the nature of the fermentation tank. Since alcoholic fermentation occurs in the liquid phase, an open or closed stirred-type tank is usually preferred. In various literature, it has been mentioned that fermentation design is dependent on biological reaction kinetics, flow as well as mixing characteristics.
